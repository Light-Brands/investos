# Step 5: Channel Adaptations

## STEP GOAL:
Collaboratively define how the brand voice and visual identity adapt across specific channels -- website, social media, email, advertising, and sales materials.

## MANDATORY EXECUTION RULES (READ FIRST):

### Universal Rules:
- NEVER generate content without user input
- CRITICAL: Read the complete step file before taking any action
- YOU ARE A FACILITATOR, not a content generator
- YOU MUST ALWAYS SPEAK OUTPUT in your Agent communication style

### Role Reinforcement:
- You are a Brand Identity Architect and facilitator
- Engage in creative dialogue about channel expression
- You bring multi-channel expertise; the user brings channel priorities

### Step-Specific Rules:
- Focus only on channel adaptations - reference prior brand work
- FORBIDDEN to look ahead to future steps

## Sequence of Instructions (Do not deviate, skip, or optimize)

### 1. Website Voice & Visual
Discuss:
- How should the brand voice adapt for the website?
- What tone works for landing pages vs. blog vs. product pages?
- What visual style governs the web experience?
- What CTAs and messaging patterns work on the site?

### 2. Social Media Adaptations
Explore per platform:
- How does the voice shift for each social platform?
- What content formats work best on each?
- How visual should social content be?
- What engagement tone should replies and comments use?

### 3. Email Voice & Style
Discuss:
- How should the brand sound in email?
- What tone works for transactional vs. marketing vs. nurture emails?
- What visual elements carry into email design?
- What subject line style fits the brand?

### 4. Advertising Voice
Explore:
- How does the brand voice adapt for paid media?
- What headline styles and hooks work?
- How does visual direction translate to ad creative?
- What differentiates brand awareness ads from conversion ads?

### 5. Sales Materials
Discuss:
- How does the brand voice apply to sales decks and proposals?
- What tone works for sales conversations?
- How do visual standards apply to sales materials?
