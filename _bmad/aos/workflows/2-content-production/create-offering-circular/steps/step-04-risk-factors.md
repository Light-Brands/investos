# Step 4: Risk Factors

## STEP GOAL:
Develop a comprehensive risk factors section that honestly and thoroughly discloses material risks to investors, organized by category with clear, plain-language descriptions.

## MANDATORY EXECUTION RULES:
- NEVER generate content without user input
- YOU ARE A FACILITATOR, not a content generator
- FORBIDDEN to look ahead to future steps
- WAIT for user confirmation before proceeding

## Sequence of Instructions

### 1. Risk Factor Framework
Explain to the user:
- Risk factors are among the most important sections of the offering document
- They must be specific to the issuer, not generic boilerplate
- Material risks must be disclosed prominently
- Risk disclosure protects both the investor and the issuer
- Reference compliance framework if available

### 2. Company-Specific Risks
Work with the user to identify and draft risks related to:
- Early-stage or limited operating history
- Dependence on key personnel
- Need for additional capital
- Customer concentration
- Technology or product risks
- Supply chain vulnerabilities
- Intellectual property risks
- Legal proceedings or disputes

### 3. Financial Risks
Identify and draft risks related to:
- Revenue uncertainty and path to profitability
- Cash flow and liquidity concerns
- Debt obligations and covenants
- Foreign currency exposure (if applicable)
- Accounting and financial reporting
- Going concern considerations

### 4. Market and Industry Risks
Identify and draft risks related to:
- Competition and market dynamics
- Regulatory changes affecting the industry
- Economic conditions and market downturns
- Technology disruption
- Customer behavior shifts
- Supply and demand fluctuations

### 5. Offering-Specific Risks
Identify and draft risks related to:
- Dilution from future issuances
- Lack of liquidity and transfer restrictions
- No public market for the securities
- Minority ownership position and limited voting rights
- Use of proceeds discretion
- Potential conflicts of interest
- Reliance on offering platform
- Tax implications

### 6. Regulatory and Legal Risks
Identify and draft risks related to:
- Changes in securities regulations
- Compliance failures and penalties
- State blue sky requirements
- Tax law changes
- Environmental or industry-specific regulations

### 7. Prioritize and Organize
Work with the user to:
- Order risk factors by materiality (most important first)
- Ensure each risk is specific, not vague or generic
- Confirm no material risks have been omitted
- Review language for clarity and accessibility

### Save Progress
Append the risk factors section to the working output file.
