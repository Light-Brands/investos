# Step 7: Financial Statements

## STEP GOAL:
Organize the financial statements section, ensuring appropriate financial information is included, properly presented, and meets the regulatory requirements for the offering type.

## MANDATORY EXECUTION RULES:
- NEVER generate content without user input
- YOU ARE A FACILITATOR, not a content generator
- FORBIDDEN to look ahead to future steps
- WAIT for user confirmation before proceeding

## Sequence of Instructions

### 1. Financial Statement Requirements
Confirm with the user the required financial statements based on regulation:
- **Reg A Tier 1**: Reviewed or audited financial statements (2 years)
- **Reg A Tier 2**: Audited financial statements (2 years)
- **Reg CF**: Financial statements based on raise amount (reviewed or audited)
- **Reg D**: No specific requirement, but best practice to include

### 2. Available Financial Information
Gather from the user:
- Are audited or reviewed financial statements available?
- Who is the independent auditor or CPA?
- What periods are covered by available financials?
- Are there interim financial statements since the last annual period?
- Are there any material events since the last financial statements?

### 3. Financial Statements to Include
Work with the user to organize:
- **Balance Sheet**: Assets, liabilities, and equity
- **Income Statement**: Revenue, expenses, and net income/loss
- **Cash Flow Statement**: Operating, investing, and financing activities
- **Statement of Changes in Equity**: Capital transactions and retained earnings
- **Notes to Financial Statements**: Accounting policies, significant items

### 4. Financial Discussion and Analysis
Guide the user through MD&A (Management Discussion and Analysis):
- Overview of financial condition and results
- Revenue drivers and trends
- Major expense categories and changes
- Liquidity and capital resources
- Known trends, events, or uncertainties
- Plan of operations (if pre-revenue or early-stage)

### 5. Capitalization Table
Document the current capitalization:
- All classes of securities outstanding
- Options, warrants, and convertible securities
- Impact of the current offering on capitalization
- Dilution analysis for new investors

### 6. Financial Formatting
Ensure proper presentation:
- Financial statements follow GAAP or specified accounting standards
- Notes are complete and referenced
- Any pro forma information is clearly labeled
- Interim statements are properly marked as unaudited (if applicable)

### Save Progress
Append the financial statements section to the working output file.
