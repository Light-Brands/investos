# Step 6: Risk Factor Disclosures

## STEP GOAL:

Develop comprehensive risk factor disclosures covering business risks, financial risks, regulatory risks, and investment-specific risks that will be included in the offering documents.

## MANDATORY EXECUTION RULES:

- NEVER generate content without user input
- YOU ARE A FACILITATOR - guide through risk identification and disclosure
- FORBIDDEN to look ahead to future steps
- Risk factors must be specific and material, not boilerplate

## Sequence of Instructions

### 1. Business & Operational Risks

Discuss with the user and identify specific risks:
- Early-stage company risks (limited operating history)
- Dependence on key personnel
- Product development and technology risks
- Competition and market risks
- Customer concentration or acquisition risks
- Supply chain or vendor dependencies
- Intellectual property risks
- Cybersecurity and data privacy risks
- Operational scalability risks
- Geographic or market-specific risks

### 2. Financial & Market Risks

Explore:
- Need for additional capital / dilution risk
- Revenue uncertainty and profitability timeline
- Cash flow and liquidity risks
- Debt obligations and leverage
- Market volatility and economic conditions
- Interest rate sensitivity
- Currency risks (if applicable)
- Valuation risks (limited comparable data)

### 3. Regulatory & Legal Risks

Discuss:
- Securities law compliance risks
- Industry-specific regulatory risks
- Litigation and legal proceedings
- Tax law changes and compliance risks
- Government policy and political risks
- Environmental regulations (if applicable)
- Licensing and permit risks
- Changes in accounting standards

### 4. Investment-Specific Risks

Detail risks specific to the offering:
- Illiquidity of securities (no public market)
- Transfer restrictions and holding period
- Lack of diversification
- Dilution from future offerings
- Conflicts of interest
- No guarantee of return or distributions
- Minority investor protections (or lack thereof)
- Leverage and debt at the entity level
- Forward-looking statement risks

### 5. Risk Prioritization & Materiality

Guide the user through:
- Ranking risks by likelihood and potential impact
- Identifying the most material risks
- Ensuring specificity (avoid generic boilerplate)
- Cross-referencing with business context for accuracy
- SEC guidance on risk factor presentation (material risks first)

### 6. Synthesize Risk Factors

Present confirmed:
- **Business Risks**: Prioritized operational and business risks
- **Financial Risks**: Market and financial condition risks
- **Regulatory Risks**: Legal and compliance risks
- **Investment Risks**: Offering-specific risk factors
- **Forward-Looking Disclaimers**: Safe harbor language framework

Ask user to confirm, correct, or expand.
